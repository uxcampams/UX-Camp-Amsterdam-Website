SECTION 5 — SESSION FORMATS
Background: #FFFFFF

Intro line above heading: Sessions at UXcampAMS come in a few different shapes: — #CCCCCC, 14px
Heading: What kinds of sessions can people run? — #333333, Extra Bold

Four format cards in a 2×2 grid (desktop), 1 column (mobile). Same card style as above (white, 1px solid #CCCCCC, 4px solid #B20101 top, no radius, padding 28px). Each card has the format name as the title in Semi Bold and the description as body text.
Card 1 — Talk / Presentation: Share knowledge on a topic you care about. Bring slides if you want, leave time for Q&A. Works best when you have a point of view, not just information.
Card 2 — Case Study: Show what you did, what worked, what didn't, and what you'd do differently. The honest ones are always the best.
Card 3 — Workshop: A hands-on session where people actually do something, apply a skill, solve a problem, make something together. Bring any specialist materials you need beyond paper and post-its.
Card 4 — Structured Discussion: Bring a frame, some questions, and a point of view. Formats like World Café or Birds of a Feather work well. The key word is structured — open-ended discussions without a guide rarely land.
Below the grid, a centered CTA link: Thinking about running one? → that links to the Running a Session page, in #B20101 Semi Bold.
