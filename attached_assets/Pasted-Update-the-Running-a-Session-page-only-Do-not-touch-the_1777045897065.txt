SECTION 3 — HOW TO RUN A SESSION
Background: #FFFFFF

Small label above heading: Step by step — #B20101, 13px, uppercase, letter-spacing 2px
Heading: How to run a session — #333333, Extra Bold, 32px

Five steps displayed as a vertical numbered flow. Each step is a full-width row with a large step number on the left (#B20101, Extra Bold, 48px, min-width 80px) and the content on the right. A 1px solid #CCCCCC horizontal line separates each step. On mobile the number sits above the content.
Step 1 — Pick a topic

Title: Pick a topic — #333333, Semi Bold, 20px
Body: Pick something you're genuinely curious or passionate about. You don't need all the answers. Ask yourself: what question do I keep coming back to? What did I learn the hard way? What would I love to debate with a room full of smart UX people?

Step 2 — Decide your goal

Title: Decide your goal — #333333, Semi Bold, 20px
Body: What do you want people to walk away with? Nail this before you prepare — it shapes everything. When you're done, check it against this goal.

Step 3 — Choose a format

Title: Choose a format — #333333, Semi Bold, 20px
Body: Talk, case study, workshop, or structured discussion. If you're not sure, go for a discussion — less pressure on you, more interesting for the room. Details on each format are on the About page.
Below the body, four small inline pills in a wrapping flex row, #F5F5F5 bg, 1px solid #CCCCCC, border-radius: 0, padding 6px 14px, 13px #333333 text: Talk · Case Study · Workshop · Structured Discussion
Below the pills, a small link: See format details → in #B20101 14px linking to the About page

Step 4 — Pitch in the Madness Session

Title: Pitch in the Madness Session — #333333, Semi Bold, 20px
Body: At 10am, grab a pitch card, join the queue, and give your 30-second pitch. Tell us: what it's about, what people will take away, and whether it's for beginners or more experienced attendees. If you pitch, you get a slot. That's the deal.
Below the body, a placeholder box: #F5F5F5 bg, 1px solid #CCCCCC, border-radius: 0, padding 20px, centered text in #CCCCCC italic 14px: 2026 pitch card template — add image here before publishing

Step 5 — Run it

Title: Run it — #333333, Semi Bold, 20px
Body: Arrive at your room a few minutes before your slot. Rooms have HDMI and USB-C, bring your own adapter if needed — we can't guarantee spares. Download anything you need beforehand; Wi-Fi can be slow. Keep an eye on time. Shorter sessions almost always land better. Break a leg.
Below the body, three small info pills same style as Step 3: HDMI available · USB-C available · Bring your own adapter
